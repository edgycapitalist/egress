Under this scenario, the full 15,000,000-share position in SPY was sold (100% filled).  Selling pushed the price from an arrival of 450.00 to 447.20 (max drawdown 1%); the position sold at a VWAP of 447.49. That cost 56 bps of implementation shortfall and 56 bps of slippage versus arrival.  No volatility halt triggered.  Full exit took 162 ticks.  Paired counterfactual impact estimates put the final-price decline at 62 bps, with approximate deltas of 47 bps from scheduled shocks, 0 bps from peer cascade, 35 bps from the exit order itself, and -20 bps residual market behaviour. Treat those as estimates, not exact causes.  The simulated mechanism is consistent with forced and panic sellers overwhelming thin bargain-hunter and market-maker support as the shocks landed: each break in the price armed the next tranche of sellers, draining the book faster than buyers replenished it.
