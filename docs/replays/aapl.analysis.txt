Under this scenario, the full 11,000,000-share position in AAPL was sold (100% filled).  Selling pushed the price from an arrival of 180.00 to 178.73 (max drawdown 1%); the position sold at a VWAP of 178.82. That cost 66 bps of implementation shortfall and 66 bps of slippage versus arrival.  No volatility halt triggered.  Full exit took 161 ticks.  Paired counterfactual impact estimates put the final-price decline at 71 bps, with approximate deltas of 56 bps from scheduled shocks, 0 bps from peer cascade, 27 bps from the exit order itself, and -12 bps residual market behaviour. Treat those as estimates, not exact causes.  The simulated mechanism is consistent with forced and panic sellers overwhelming thin bargain-hunter and market-maker support as the shocks landed: each break in the price armed the next tranche of sellers, draining the book faster than buyers replenished it.
