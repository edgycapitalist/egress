Under this scenario, the exit did not fully close: only 1% of the 2,400,000-share position in CVNA could be sold, leaving 2,371,200 shares (99%) stuck.  Selling pushed the price from an arrival of 15.00 to 7.50 (max drawdown 52%); the position sold at a VWAP of 14.78. That cost 150 bps of implementation shortfall and 150 bps of slippage versus arrival.  A volatility halt triggered 3 time(s), pausing trading and worsening the stuck position.  The position never fully exited.  Paired counterfactual impact estimates put the final-price decline at 5000 bps, with approximate deltas of 4953 bps from scheduled shocks, 0 bps from peer cascade, 0 bps from the exit order itself, and 47 bps residual market behaviour. Treat those as estimates, not exact causes.  The simulated mechanism is consistent with forced and panic sellers overwhelming thin bargain-hunter and market-maker support as the shocks landed: each break in the price armed the next tranche of sellers, draining the book faster than buyers replenished it.
