# Peer Crowding And Correlated Exits

Peer crowding is separate from the behavioural crowding mix. The peer profile
describes similar funds holding the same trade, their likely overlap, the drawdown
or risk trigger they may share, and the probability that they exit together.

Free/public inputs such as 13F, curated episode fixtures, user-uploaded holdings,
and synthetic assumptions can support this profile, but the system must label
source and confidence. A low/base/high ensemble is more honest than a single
point estimate because the true overlap is usually uncertain.
